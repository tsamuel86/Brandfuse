From the magical experience of trailing your fingertips through glowing water on bioluminescent bays under the moon to walking on long stretches of almond-colored sand at sunset, you’ll find Puerto Rico literally dripping with romance. Add the sophisticated European flavor of the island’s cities to the mix, with the advantage of being in the center of the Caribbean.

If enchantment is your aim, Puerto Rico delivers. Not only do outdoor activities for fun togetherness and spots for languid relaxation and bonding abound, but this island territory also offers travel styles to suit any taste or budget. Plus, getting to Puerto Rico is super easy: no passport required for U.S. citizens and more than 400 weekly flights from U.S. cities.

Planning

Puerto Rico is an idyllic blend of world-class attractions, magnificent natural wonders and Old World allure. The capital city, San Juan, is the island’s most visited destination, serving nearly a dozen cruise lines. Large by Caribbean island standards, Puerto Rico has 272 miles of coastline. 

Old San Juan, the Caribbean’s only walled city, offers prime shopping with craft markets selling traditional wares like papier mache masks, mundillo lace and small wooden figurines of saints. Meanwhile diners in San Juan will find eclectic cuisine mingling Spanish, Creole and Taíno indian traditions.

One of the best ways to spend a day here is exploring the tunnels, stairs and bastions of San Juan’s four Spanish Colonial fortresses. Among them is the Castillo de San Felipe del Morro, which offers stunning vistas over San Juan Bay.

 A United Nations World Heritage Site, Old San Juan’s rich historic architecture includes the medieval Catedral de San Juan, which houses Spanish explorer Ponce de Leon’s tomb, and the 16th-century La Fortaleza, the oldest continually used executive mansion in the Western Hemisphere. Take a stroll through the blue cobblestone streets of Old San Juan, winding in and out of the stores, bistros and cafes along the way. Another great place to stroll: Paseo del Morro, which offers views of the city walls and the cerulean bay.

Less than 30 miles from San Juan is the only tropical rainforest in the U.S. National Forest system, El Yunque Rainforest. Enriched with heavy annual precipitation, the forest includes 240 tree species and is a netherworld of waterfalls and placid pools.

You’ll find equally inspiring portraits of paradise along Puerto Rico’s award-winning beaches, which number in the hundreds.

An often overlooked Caribbean dive spot, Puerto Rico offers scuba diving couples a chance to explore rare black coral reefs. And few things are as enchanting as Puerto Rico’s three bioluminescent bays, where endless amounts of microscopic, single-celled organisms light up like stardust whenever activated by movement. And all three of them — Mosquito Bay in Vieques, Laguna Grande in Fajardo and La Perguera in Lajas — are easily accessible all year-round. 

And through the end of January, Puerto Rico’s calendar is loaded with festivities and events ranging from Despedida de Año parties to ring in 2017 to the San Sebastian Street Festival in Old San Juan.

Where to Stay

Romantic accommodations abound to fit every budget and need. If you’re seeking the ultimate in luxury and service, you’ll find hotels exuding Spanish Revival architecture and Roaring Twenties glamor. Some offer 24-hour butler service and private pools, and many provide sumptuous ocean views from expansive suites.

Or you can completely get away from it all and choose a secluded lodge or casita with no televisions but plenty of relaxation while still maintaining proximity to serene beaches and maybe even regular views of wild horses.

Where to Eat

Puerto Rico’s culinary scene ranges from white tablecloth experiences to casual dining right on the beach. The island’s cuisine features influences of Spain, Africa and the native Taíno, and is famous for its complex rice dishes and stews like arroz con dulce and asopao.

Salivate over menus showcasing marinated cassava, green banana fritters or cornstarch custard drizzled with butter and spiced with cinnamon. Fried plantains, delicious flatbreads, sweet and sour combinations and fruits and vegetables like breadfruit and avocado are common in Puerto Rican cooking.

Foodies will find plenty to please their please their palates in Old San Juan, La Placita de Santurce, Condado, Rincon and Vieques.

Sample Itineraries for Two

Few islands in the Caribbean are as accessible or as romantic, and U.S. citizens can travel to Puerto Rico without a passport, with nonstop flights arriving in San Juan from several major U.S. cities.

If you have only three or four days, stay in or close to San Juan to maximize vacation time. The capital has a variety of sightseeing and activities, and it’s possible to get around without renting a car.

If you have five days or more to explore, head to Rincón. This municipality, as well as Isabela and Aguadilla offer superior surfing as well as scuba, snorkeling, kayaking and whale watching in winter months. Visit the Lajas beach towns of Cabo Rojo, Guanica and La Parguera for views of burnt red sea cliffs, visits to distilleries and wineries, nature reserves and coral reefs. Be sure to take advantage of direct flights to the Aguadilla airport.

Paying

While the diversity of activities makes travel to Puerto Rico attractive to couples, so does the price. Travelers can find getaways to the Isle of Enchantment on any budget, with moderately priced accommodations available for about $100 a night, and more decadent lodgings for $700 a night.

Puerto Rico is also an affordable getaway at a moment’s notice. Currently, it’s possible to save up to 60 percent on a range of hotels on the island, in case you’re in the mood for a balmy respite from the teeth-shattering winter.

Wondering what to pack for your Puerto Rican holiday?
Consider the activities you’ll want to enjoy:
Puerto Rico’s climate is tropical, so pack light, summertime clothing and shoes, as well as a light jacket or sweater for cooler evenings. The island enjoys an average year-round temperature of 85 degrees Fahrenheit.
If your plans involve lots of sunbathing and beach time, pack your swimsuit, sunscreen, a hat, sunglasses and beach bag. Most oceanfront resorts offer beach chairs, umbrellas and watersport equipment to rent.
If you want to hike in El Yunque Rainforest, you might want to consider closed-toe shoes or hiking boots, plus sunscreen, insect repellant, a hat, raincoat and a sturdy bottle for water. Water shoes and a bathing suit are a good idea for an impromptu dip in one of the rainforest pools.
If you’re going to experience Puerto Rico both on and under the water,  you’ll need a swimsuit or quick-drying clothing with water shoes for kayaking. Also bring a hat, sunscreen and insect repellant, and your kayak wear will work for boat charters as well. Many outfitters offer equipment rentals for diving and snorkeling, but if you bring your own gear, don’t forget your dive mask, snorkel, flippers and a wetsuit if you’re planning to scuba dive.
If you’re hoping to sample Puerto Rico’s nightlife, stow some light slacks and a collared shirt or a sundress in your luggage.
